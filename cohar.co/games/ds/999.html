999: Nine Hours, Nine Persons, Nine Doors

Recently I've been making an effort to play some of my lesser used platforms. I have a number of titles for the original Nintendo DS, but often opt to play the 3DS instead. I figured I'd mix things up for a change and give 999 a try, as I've always heard great things. The game is ultimately a visual novel with some puzzle elements, and drops you right into the action as you wake up on a seemingly sinking ship. From there, the story unfurls as your character Junpei seeks to answer the plethora of looming questions. While it is missing some of the luxuries from modern games in the genre, like voice acting, 999 is able to present a unique and interesting story that remains intriguing throughout. The story is told neatly across multiple playthroughs in which you are miraculously capable of remembering important events and details between each one. There are six different endings depending on the choices you make during your journey - the first ending I got was very surprising, and had me immediately starting a new one to find another path. After your first run thru, you are able to fast-forward through dialog that you have already experienced. I would have liked to see more options for skipping dialogue, as fast-forwarding still ended up being a time consuming endeavor anyways. The puzzles scattered throughout are solid, and offer interesting insights into the characters and story as you progress through them. I wish that there was a little more complexity in some of the puzzles, but I'm glad that the existing puzzles were sensible at the very least. What immediately struck me about 999 was the soundtrack - despite relying on the meager Nintendo DS the game offers some excellent accompaniments to your investigations and puzzle solving. Without providing any spoilers, the story culminates quite nicely while also leaving some room for the sequels. Overall, this is a game I could easily recommend to a fan of the visual novel genre, as the story and characters alone are interesting enough to warrant playing. That being said, it probably won't make you a fan of the genre if you aren't one already.

Play it for the:

  • Mysterious and fitting electronic soundtrack
  • Subversive and exciting narrative
  • Simple but rewarding point and click puzzles
