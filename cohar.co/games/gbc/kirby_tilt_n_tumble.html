Kirby Tilt 'n' Tumble

The next game in my Game Boy Color and coffee routine, Kirby Tilt 'n' Tumble, required some locking-in. Featuring an accelerometer built into the translucent pink cartridge, this iteration of Kirby has you tilting your console in order to move through top-down levels until you reach your goal. This may be the first game to leverage gyroscopic controls, and in hindsight seems like foreshadowing for Nintendo's eventual obsession with the concept in the form of the Wii. The gameplay here is very simple, but quite challenging due to the nature of the hardware. As a rolling version of Kirby, you make your way through eight different levels around Planet Popstar, each with four sub-levels. Each level varies in terms of their difficulty and quality, and the final level of each set of four culminates in a boss battle. Aside from tilting, you can also shake the console to make Kirby jump - this typically ruins your ability to control, but thankfully lightly tapping the console seemed to reliably induce a jump without much chaos. Along with the goal, each level features a number of collectibles including a Red Star. These stars are often very tough to get, requiring precise movement and jumping. I only ended the game with four out of a possible thirty-two Red Stars, so completing that collection would surely present a great challenge. Overall I found the gameplay to have a great balance of difficulty, although at times things did feel unfair. Kirby Tilt 'n' Tumble is an oddity of a game, and provides evidence that hardware limitations can sometimes be used as a catalyst for innovation. As a piece of gaming history, and as a genuinely fun game, this Kirby entry should definitely not be forgotten.

Play it for the:

  • Cute rolling Kirby sprite
  • Challenging tilt-based platforming
  • Surprising replayability
